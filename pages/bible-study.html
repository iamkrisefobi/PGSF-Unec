Study to shew thyself approved unto God, a workman that needeth not to be ashamed, rightly dividing the word of truth - 2 Timothy 2:15

Topic: The Word of God

Text: Heb 4:12, 2 Tim 3:16-17

Introduction

The word of God is the intent, will and purpose of God revealed to mankind. It is also a medium of communication from God to man. The word of God is a manual for daily living for believers and beyond that; the word of God is God himself (Jn 1:1). It is the basic element for the equipping of the saints for good works (II Tim 3:16-17).

Discussion Questions

  1. What is the word of God? Jn 1:1-4; 14, Jn 6:63; Jn 17:17, Eph 6:17,Heb 4:12.
  2. What are the attributes of the word? Jn 6:63, Heb 11:3, 4:12-13, I Pet 1:23; 25, I Thes 2:13, Prov 30:5, Jer 23:29, Acts 19:20
  3. How do I benefit from the word? Jas 1:21-23, Heb 11:6,8, Josh 1:8, Psalm 119:9-11
  4. What are the Barriers to benefitting from the word? Heb 4:2, Matt 13:18-22, Eph 4:17-18, II Cor 3:14-16
  5. What are the benefits of the word? Psalm 119:9, 11,105,130, Isa 30:21, II Cor 9:8, Luke 5:5-6, Psalm 112:1-3, II Cor 3:18, Rev 1:3

Conclusion

The word of God plays an indispensable role in the life of believers. The study of the word is pivotal to the equipping of the saints for good works, because the word enables us to be modeled after Christ. Believers are therefore invited to not only be hearers alone, but also doers of the word.

Memory Verse

For the word of God is living and powerful and sharper than any two edged sword piercing even to the division of soul and spirit and of joints and marrow, and is a discerner of the thoughts and intents of the heart. (Heb 4:16) NKJV

Topic: Efficacy of the word of God

Text: 2 Timothy 3:16-17, Psalms 119:130

Introduction

The word of God is powerful, pure and of great value. The word of God is able to keep a man from sin (Psa.119:9-11). Efficacy implies the ability to produce a desired or an intended result. The word of God has the ability to accomplish the set goal of which God sent it to accomplish (Psa.107:20). In this study, we are trusting that God will grant us understanding on the efficacy of his word.

Discussion Questions

  1. What is the word of God? (Gen. 1:1-24, John 1:1-5,14, Heb 4:12-13, Psalm 119:105, 2 Tim 3:16-17)
  2. What is the efficacy of the word of God? (Psa. 19:7, Psa. 119:9,11, 107:20, II Tim. 3:16-17, Heb. 4:12)
  3. What are the dangers of taking the Word of God for granted? (John 3:19, Psalm 50:16-17, Isa. 5:1-7, Rom 1:28, Neh 9:26-27)
  4. What is the joy of taking heed to the Word of God? (John 15:7, Prov. 3:1-4, Prov. 2:1-6, Psalm 119:11, Rev. 22:11-13)

Conclusion

The word of God is not an opinion of any man neither is it a compilation of men's philosophies and fables. Rather, it is the spoken mind of God (II Peter1:20-21). Therefore, the Word of God is sure and irrevocable thus we can base and invest our lives on it.

The world and everything in it came to existence via the word of God so put your trust solely on it for it's all you need to go through life and get to heaven.

Memory Verse

Sanctify them [purify, consecrate, separate them for Yourself, make them holy] by the Truth; Your Word is Truth (John 17:17 AMP)

Topic: Consecration

Text: Exodus 32:29, Judges 13:3-7

Introduction

Consecration is a process or practice of making something or someone sacred, dedicated and set apart for the Lord. It also means the devoting or setting apart of anything or anyone to the worship or service of God (Exodus 28:41). The race of Abraham and the levites were thus consecrated (Num 3:12). According to the Mosaic Law, the firstborn male of both man and beast were consecrated to God (Exo 13:2, 12, 15). In the New Testament, Christians are regarded as consecrated to the Lord (I Pet 2:9, Rom 12:1)

Discussion Questions

  1. What does it mean to consecrate ourselves to God? Josh 3:5, Rom 12:1, Exo 13:2, 19:10,22, Exo 30:30, 32:29, Rom 6:13, Heb 12:14, Judges 13:3-7
  2. What is broken consecration and discuss some examples? Judges 14;1-3, 8-9, Num 12:1;10, Num 20:7-12, II Sam 11:1-17, Judges 8:22-27
  3. Means of attaining consecration Rom 12:2, II Chron 17:16, II Cor 7:1, II Tim 2:2, 20-21, Jn 17:17, Psalm 119:9
  4. What are the benefits of consecration? Exo 32:29, Heb 1:9, II Tim 2:21, Acts 5:12, matt 28:19-20, Psalm 91:1, Prov 16:7, II Chron 20:15-19

Conclusion

As children of God and citizens of his kingdom, God expects us to live a life of purity, holiness and consecration to him as a peculiar people and a royal priesthood (I pet 2:9). God desires that we separate ourselves from the world (Rom 12:2) and consecrate ourselves solely for his purposes on earth.

Memory Verse

Then Moses said, “Consecrate yourselves today to the Lord that he may bestow on you a blessing this day”. (Exo 32:29)

Topic: Separation

Text: 2 Cor. 6 vs 16-18

Introduction

Separation is from the word 'to separate', and to separate connotes being different, unique, and apart and being seen in a different light. Because of the call of God upon our lives, and the demand of God upon us as His dear children, there are so many things we cannot go with. Actually, the truth is, others may, but we cannot. (1 Pet. 2 vs 9)

Discussion Questions

  1. What do you understand by the term separation?
  2. What are we called to separate from? Rom. 12 vs 2, 2 Cor. 6 vs 17, 1 Pet. 2 vs 1, 1 Jhn. 2 vs 15-17, 1 Pet. 4 vs 1-4, 1 Pet. 3 vs 8-11
  3. What are the consequences of lack of separation Jas. 4 vs 4-5, Jhn. 15 vs 6, 1 Pet. 3 vs 12, 2 Pet. 2 vs 4-6, 13-15, 2 Pet. 3 vs 9-14
  4. What are the benefits of separation John. 15 vs 7-10, 2 Cor. 6 vs 17-18

Conclusion

As children of God, we are called to be the light and salt (Matt. 5 vs 13-16), to live not as the world live (Rom. 12 vs 1-2), but to live a life that gives pleasure to God at all times (Rev. 4 vs 11). We cannot live this life except we separate ourselves unto God alone. When we separate ourselves to God, we get his blessing, but when we refused to, we get his wrath. May God grant us grace and understanding to know and do his bidding. God bless us all.

Memory Verse

Wherefore come out from them and be ye separate, saith the Lord, and touch not the unclean thing; and I will receive you. (2 Cor. 6 vs 17)
